    How CC Analyzer works

    The flow

    1. Upload your CC PDF + cutoff date + Money Manager source.
    2. Server parses the PDF, fetches MM entries for the billing cycle, and reconciles them.
    3. Review the flagged rows, fix MM on your phone, and hit Re-analyze to re-check.
    4. When everything reconciles, hit Confirm & Push to Sheet to write a new worksheet.

    Status labels (CC Transactions table)

    • matched Found an MM entry with the same amount inside the CC period — picks the nearest by date when several have the same amount. Nothing to do.
    • inaccurate No exact-amount entry inside the CC period, but one or more within ±5% amount drift. Candidates are ordered nearest-by-date first; pick the right one, manually link, or add manually.
    • missing No MM entry inside the CC period is within ±5% of the CC amount. Link to one from the MM list or add manually.

    "CC period" = the date range of the PDF's transactions (min..max tran date), padded ±3 days to absorb MM Period drift. The cycle pool below may extend slightly beyond this — those entries don't enter automatic matching, but you can still pick them via the manual-pick dropdown.

    Resolve column (right-most)

    For each non-matched row, options are presented in fall-through order:

    1. Candidate checkboxes (inaccurate only) — algorithm-suggested MM entries. Tick the right one.
    2. Pick from MM list dropdown — every MM entry in the cycle. Use this if the algorithm missed it.
    3. To be added manually — fall-through. No MM entry exists; you'll create one.

    Within a row, picking any option clears the others. Picking an MM entry in row A locks it from being picked in row B (and strikes it through in the bottom table).

    Money Manager lookup window

    The bottom Money Manager CC entries table is anchored to the billing cycle: cutoff − 1 month … cutoff − 1 day. The cutoff date itself belongs to the next bill, not this one. We pad the upper edge by 1 day to catch MM exports that occasionally drift +1 day forward — so a sheet entry on the cutoff day still appears as the bill's last day. The lower edge stays strict to avoid pulling in pre-cycle entries that drift forward into the cycle start.

    The MM Period column is what we display. The internal matching uses Period − 1 day to absorb a known one-day drift between MM and the bank.

    How candidates are picked

    For each non-matched CC tx we scan every entry in the cycle pool. An MM entry becomes a candidate via either path:

    • Close on amount — within ±5% of the CC amount, regardless of merchant.
    • Merchant hit — token overlap between the CC merchant string (e.g. STARBUCKS MANILA) and the MM expense name + description ≥ 70%, AND amount within ±50% of the CC. The amount cap stops a strong merchant overlap from pulling in a wildly different value (e.g. 173 vs 2,729).

    Generic location words (manila, makati, etc.) are filtered out before the merchant comparison so they don't trigger false positives. Candidates display earliest-by-date for consistency with the other tables.

    Excluding MM entries

    The leftmost column on the MM table has an Exclude checkbox. Tick it for any entry that doesn't actually belong to this bill (e.g. a duplicate, a misposted cycle, or an entry you'll deal with separately).

    An excluded entry:

    • Disappears from candidate checkboxes and manual-pick dropdowns.
    • Is added to the punch list as a Remove action so you delete it from MM.
    • Drops from the projected MM count and total in the Verification panel.

    The bottom MM table is a review process — leftover entries are never auto-removed. You decide per entry whether it belongs on this bill (link it / leave it) or not (exclude it).

    Action types in the punch list

    • Add Create a new MM entry from the CC tx (you ticked "To be added manually").
    • Update Change an existing MM entry's amount to match CC.
    • Match The MM entry already equals CC — no MM change needed, just acknowledged.
    • Remove You ticked the Exclude checkbox on an MM entry — it doesn't belong on this bill, delete it from MM. Removal is never automatic; review each leftover entry yourself.

    Shoulder Summary

    Tag a Money Manager note with one or more @Name mentions and the bottom Shoulder summary panel splits that CC amount equally among the tagged people.

    • Example: Lunch with team @shane @christian on a 600 CC tx → @shane = 300, @christian = 300.
    • Updates live as you tick candidate checkboxes or pick from the manual dropdown — picking an MM entry with tags adds them to the totals.
    • "Add" actions don't contribute (no MM entry yet, no tags to read).

    Verification panel

    As you tick resolves, the panel projects what your MM totals will look like after you apply the punch list. The diff card turns green when:

    • Projected MM count = CC count, AND
    • Projected MM total = CC total (within 1 cent).

    If they don't match, fix the discrepancy in MM, hit Re-analyze, and try again.

    Re-analyze vs Confirm & Push

    • Re-analyze (Google Sheet source only) — re-fetches MM data with the same PDF, no re-upload. Use it as many times as you want.
    • Confirm & Push — writes a new worksheet to the destination Google Sheet. Don't push until the Verification panel is green.

    Due date

    Cutoff + 21 days, bumped past weekends. If the bumped weekday is a public holiday, the actual banking day will be later — that part isn't modelled. Display only; it does not affect the lookup window.
